# LGBTQ+ Rights in China

## Homosexuality
Homosexual relations have been legal since 1997 ([*Penal Code of 1997*](https://www.oecd.org/site/adboecdanti-corruptioninitiative/46814270.pdf)).

## Gender Transition
Sex-reassignment surgery is required to [change one's gender identity on legal documentation](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/19361653.2010.512518) since 2009.

## Same-Sex Marriage
Same-sex relationships have not been recognized in this country since 1980 ([*Zhōnghuá Rénmín Gòng Héguó Hūnyīn Fǎ*](https://web.archive.org/web/20161122062434/http://www.npc.gov.cn/englishnpc/Law/2007-12/13/content_1384064.htm)).
* **Beijing** provides dependent residency status to [same-sex partners of legal residents](https://archive.vn/20190617191301/lexuniversal.com/en/news/16983).
* **Hong Kong** [protects same-sex couples](https://www.pinknews.co.uk/2009/06/04/gay-couples-to-be-protected-by-hong-kong-domestic-violence-law/) under its *Domestic Violence Ordinance* (*Jiātíng Jí Tóngjū Guānxi Bàolì Tiáolì*). Additionally, same-sex partners of government employees in Hong Kong [must receive the same benefits](https://hongkongfp.com/2019/06/06/breaking-hong-kong-top-court-grants-spousal-benefits-husband-gay-civil-servant/) as opposite-sex partners.

## Same-Sex Adoption
The Chinese Government [does not allow same-sex adoptions](https://web.archive.org/web/20110323005828/http://adoption.state.gov/country/china.html#who1).

## Military
There are no known laws allowing LGBT+ citizens to openly serve in the military. However, it also not explicitly prohibited.

## Discrimination Laws
There are no specific anti-discrimination laws protecting LGBT+ citizens in this country. However, [Article 33 of the Chinese Constitution](http://www.npc.gov.cn/zgrdw/englishnpc/Constitution/node_2825.htm) states, "All citizens of the People’s Republic of China are equal before the law."
* **Hong Kong** has interpreted its *Hong Kong Bill of Rights Ordinance 1991* to include sexual orientation as a protected category ([*Xiānggǎng Rénquán Fǎ'àn Tiáolì*](https://www.cmab.gov.hk/doc/en/documents/policy_responsibilities/the_rights_of_the_individuals/human/BORO-InductoryChapterandBooklet-Eng.pdf)).
